About Mamo Gardens Model Farm Initiative

Cultivating sustainable futures since 2016

Mamo Gardens Model Farm Initiative is a registered Community Based Organization (CBO) dedicated to transforming the agricultural landscape of Northern Uganda. Since our establishment in 2016, we have been at the forefront of promoting climate-resilient agriculture and environmental conservation in Nebbi District and beyond.

Our Vision

A Clean Uganda where sustainable food system and environmental conservation through resilient farming practices are a culture.

Our Mission

To improve the livelihoods of marginalized farmer households growing field crops and forages, increase their production and productivity, and improve environmental conservation through climate-smart and resilient farming.

Our Goal

To enhance the socio-economic wellbeing of marginalized and poor farmer communities through innovative agricultural practices and community-driven development.

Tree Planting and Agroforestry Practices

Building Sustainable Agricultural Ecosystems

Our Mission

At Mamo Gardens Model Farm Initiative, we're revolutionizing agriculture in Nebbi District through comprehensive agroforestry practices that restore ecosystems while boosting farm productivity.

Why Agroforestry Matters

Agroforestry represents the future of sustainable farming, combining trees with crops and livestock to create resilient agricultural systems. Our approach addresses climate change, soil degradation, and food security simultaneously.

Our Agroforestry Techniques

Alley Cropping

Integrating fruit trees with annual crops to maximize land use and provide multiple income streams.

Windbreaks

Strategic tree placement to protect crops from wind damage and reduce soil erosion.

Living Fences

Using indigenous trees as natural boundaries while providing additional benefits like fruits and timber.

Nitrogen Fixation

Planting leguminous trees that naturally enrich soil fertility and reduce fertilizer dependency.

Impact on Local Community

Our tree planting initiatives have transformed the agricultural landscape of Nebbi District. Farmers report increased crop yields, improved soil health, and diversified income sources. The program has also created employment opportunities for local youth in nursery management and tree care.

Key Achievements

  • • Established 10 community tree nurseries
  • • Trained 200+ farmers in agroforestry techniques
  • • Increased average farm income by 35%
  • • Improved soil organic matter by 40%
  • • Reduced soil erosion by 60% on participating farms

Looking Forward

We're expanding our agroforestry program to reach 500 more farmers over the next two years. Our goal is to establish Nebbi District as a model for climate-smart agriculture in Uganda, demonstrating how traditional farming can evolve to meet modern environmental challenges while maintaining profitability.

Nutrition and Fruit Growing Workshop

Empowering Communities Through Nutritious Food Production

Partnership Excellence

In collaboration with the District Nutrition Team, we organized a comprehensive workshop that bridges the gap between nutrition science and practical fruit cultivation, empowering farmers with knowledge to grow nutrient-rich foods.

Workshop Overview

This intensive three-day workshop brought together nutritionists, agricultural experts, and local farmers to explore the connection between fruit cultivation and community health. Participants learned both the science of nutrition and practical skills for sustainable fruit production.

Key Workshop Components

Nutritional Education

Comprehensive training on vitamin content, mineral composition, and health benefits of various fruits.

Cultivation Techniques

Hands-on demonstrations of planting, pruning, pest management, and harvesting best practices.

Value Addition

Practical sessions on juice making, drying, and preservation to extend shelf life and increase income.

Market Analysis

Understanding market demands, pricing strategies, and creating sustainable business models.

Fruits Covered in Workshop

The workshop featured detailed sessions on cultivating and processing various fruits suitable for Nebbi District's climate:

Tropical Fruits
  • • Mangoes (3 varieties)
  • • Papayas
  • • Pineapples
  • • Passion fruits
Citrus & Others
  • • Oranges & Lemons
  • • Avocados
  • • Grapes
  • • Indigenous fruits

Workshop Outcomes

  • • 85% of participants established home fruit gardens
  • • 12 community juice-making groups formed
  • • Average household fruit consumption increased by 60%
  • • 40 participants started small fruit processing businesses
  • • Malnutrition rates in participating communities decreased by 25%

Community Impact

The workshop's impact extends beyond individual farms to transform entire communities. Families now have access to fresh, nutritious fruits year-round, while women have found new income opportunities through fruit processing cooperatives.

Future Plans

Building on this success, we're planning quarterly nutrition workshops, establishing a mobile fruit processing unit, and creating a certification program for local nutrition champions. Our goal is to make Nebbi District a model for nutrition-focused agriculture in Uganda.

Grapevine Cultivation Center

Excellence in Viticulture

Our grapevine yard represents a pioneering effort in Uganda's wine industry, combining traditional winemaking knowledge with modern sustainable practices to produce premium wines while training the next generation of viticulturists.

Our Vineyard

Spanning 15 acres of carefully managed terroir, our vineyard showcases how grape cultivation can thrive in Uganda's unique climate. We've adapted international best practices to local conditions, creating a model for sustainable wine production in East Africa.

Grape Varieties

We cultivate both international and adapted varieties, each selected for their performance in our local climate:

Red Varieties

Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Sangiovese adapted for tropical conditions.

White Varieties

Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, and local white varieties producing crisp, fresh wines.

Table Grapes

Testing new cultivars for climate adaptation and disease resistance.
